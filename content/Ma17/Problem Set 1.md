6. Prove that $\frac{21n+4}{14n+3}$ is irreducible for every $n \in \mathbb{N}$.

`\begin{proof}` Using Euclidean Algorithm, we get

$$
\begin{align*}
	\text{gcd}(21n+4, 14n+3) &= \text{gcd}(14n+3, 7n+1) \\
	&= \text{gcd}(7n+1, 1) \\
	&= 1
.\end{align*}
$$

Thus, the fraction is irreducible.
`\end{proof}`
